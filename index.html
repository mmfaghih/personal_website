Work

I have so far worked on variety of projects, which has given me both numerical as well as experiemental research skills.

During my PhD, I designed and implemented an experimental set up to freeze the motion of fast-moving human red blood cells under different fluid stresses caused by different channel geometries. Also, one of my projects at US FDA involved setting up mechanical and electrical experiemts to detect micron-sized holes in Surgical Tissue Containment Systems.

During my postdoc, I used OpenFOAM to simulate blood flow in IVCs of male vs female patients, in order to analyze the gender-specifc performance of IVF filters.

See my Google Scholar profile to see list of my publications.
